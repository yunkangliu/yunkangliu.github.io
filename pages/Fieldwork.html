Industrial Development,Maritime Trade Infrastructure, and Environment/Social Changes in Thailand

7-8 Nov 2019 – The Map Ta Phut industrial estate in Rayong, (Thailand)

Map Ta Phut Industrial Estate in Rayong, Thailand, is the largest petrochemical complex in Southeast Asia and has played a key role in the country’s industrialization under the Eastern Seaboard development strategy. Built after the discovery of natural gas in the Gulf of Thailand, the complex connects closely with major infrastructure such as Laem Chabang Port and supports Thailand’s manufacturing sector. However, its development has generated significant environmental and social controversies, including pollution, health concerns, land disputes, and limited local employment benefits. Although the government and companies have introduced initiatives such as eco-industrial planning, environmental monitoring and CSR programs, the relationship between industry and local communities remains contested.

  • Visits to Map Ta Phut Industrial Estate and siam cement group (SCG) group.
  • Focus group study on relevant stakeholders and local community development around industrial parks.

Migrant Workers, Disadvantaged Groups, and Fishing Communities in Southeast Asia

20-30 Oct 2020 – Bangkok, Khlong Toei and Samutsakorn (Thailand)

This fieldwork explores the social and developmental challenges faced by migrant workers and disadvantaged communities in mainland Southeast Asia, with a particular focus on Thailand. As regional economic integration deepens and labor mobility increases, migrant workers have become an essential component of the region’s economic development. However, many migrant communities continue to face structural challenges, particularly in relation to education access, skills development, and labor protection.

  • Dear Burma School (Development of Education and Awareness for Refugees from Burma), Bangkok – Visit and discussion with educators on migrant education and community learning initiatives for Myanmar migrant children.
  • Duang Prateep Foundation, Khlong Toei, Bangkok – Engagement with community development practitioners working with disadvantaged urban communities and migrant families.
  • Labour Protection Network (LPN), Samut Sakhon Province, Thailand – Field research with an NGO supporting migrant workers and fishing communities, focusing on labor rights, education, and social protection.

Gender, Social Protection, and Women's Empowerment in Thailand

12 November 2019 – Association for the Promotion of the Status of Women (under the Royal Patronage of HRH Princess Soamsawali Krom Muen Suddhanarinatha), Bangkok, Thailand

This field visit examined the role of civil society organizations in promoting gender equality, social protection, and the empowerment of disadvantaged women in Thailand. The research focuses on how non-governmental organizations contribute to social welfare provision, vocational training, and community development, particularly for women facing economic and social vulnerabilities.

  • Visit and discussion with organizational representatives on programs supporting women's empowerment, vocational training, and social services for disadvantaged women and families.
